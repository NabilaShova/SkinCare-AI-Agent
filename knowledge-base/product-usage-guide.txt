Glow Beauty Co. — Product Usage Guide

General Layering Order (Thinnest to Thickest)
1. Cleanser
2. Toner or essence (if used)
3. Water-based serums (hyaluronic acid, niacinamide)
4. Treatment serums (Vitamin C morning, retinol or BHA night)
5. Moisturizer
6. Sunscreen (morning only)

Morning Routine Example
1. Gentle cleanser — remove overnight oil and prep skin.
2. Vitamin C serum — antioxidant protection; supports brightening.
3. Lightweight moisturizer — hydrate without heaviness.
4. SPF 50 sunscreen — essential daily protection.

Night Routine Example
1. Cleanser — remove sunscreen, makeup, and daily buildup.
2. Treatment serum — niacinamide for oil control, or BHA for pores/acne, or retinol for aging concerns.
3. Moisturizer — repair and seal hydration overnight.

Oily and Acne-Prone Skin
- Look for oil-free gel moisturizers and niacinamide or salicylic acid serums.
- Avoid heavy occlusive creams unless skin is also dehydrated.
- Do not over-exfoliate; start 2-3 nights per week for acids/retinol.

Dry and Sensitive Skin
- Prioritize gentle cleansers, ceramide moisturizers, and fragrance-free formulas.
- Introduce actives slowly.
- Patch test new products behind the ear or on the jaw for 24-48 hours.

Combination Skin
- You may use lighter products on the T-zone and richer products on dry areas.
- Niacinamide is versatile for balancing combination skin.

How Often to Use Actives
- Vitamin C: most mornings.
- Niacinamide: once or twice daily as tolerated.
- Retinol: start 2-3 nights weekly, increase gradually.
- Salicylic acid (BHA): start 2-3 nights weekly; do not combine with retinol on the same night when beginning.

Patch Testing
- Apply a small amount to the inner arm or behind the ear.
- Wait 24-48 hours before full-face use if you have sensitive skin.

When to Stop and Seek Help
- Burning, swelling, hives, or worsening rash — discontinue use and see a dermatologist.
