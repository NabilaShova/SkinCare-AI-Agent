Glow Beauty Co. — Skin Types Guide (Evidence-Based)

Purpose
Help the AI advisor identify skin type patterns and recommend appropriate product categories and ingredients. This is general skincare education, not medical diagnosis.

How to Identify Skin Type
- Oily: shine on forehead, nose, and chin by midday; makeup may break down; pores often appear more visible.
- Dry: tight feeling after cleansing; flaking or rough patches; fine lines may look more pronounced when dehydrated.
- Combination: oily T-zone (forehead, nose, chin) with normal or dry cheeks.
- Sensitive: stinging, burning, or redness with new products; reacts easily to fragrance, alcohol, or strong acids.
- Normal: balanced moisture, few visible concerns, tolerates most products without irritation.

Oily Skin — Best Practices
Characteristics: excess sebum, enlarged-appearing pores, acne-prone tendency, makeup slippage.
Goals: regulate oil without stripping, keep pores clear, prevent breakouts, use non-comedogenic formulas.
Recommended ingredients: niacinamide (2-10%), salicylic acid/BHA (0.5-2%), zinc PCA, hyaluronic acid (lightweight hydration), tea tree (spot use), clay (masks 1x weekly).
Product textures: gel cleanser, lightweight gel moisturizer, oil-free serums, mineral or gel sunscreen.
Avoid: heavy occlusive balms on full face, over-cleansing (more than twice daily), harsh physical scrubs on active breakouts.
Morning formula: gentle gel cleanser → niacinamide or antioxidant serum → oil-free moisturizer → broad-spectrum SPF 30+.
Night formula: cleanser (double cleanse if sunscreen/makeup) → BHA or niacinamide treatment → lightweight moisturizer.

Dry Skin — Best Practices
Characteristics: low sebum, compromised barrier feel, rough texture, sensitivity to harsh surfactants.
Goals: restore barrier, lock in moisture, minimize transepidermal water loss, introduce actives slowly.
Recommended ingredients: ceramides, cholesterol, fatty acids, hyaluronic acid, glycerin, squalane, panthenol, shea butter (rich creams).
Product textures: cream or milk cleanser, hydrating toner, serum, rich cream moisturizer, SPF with moisturizing base.
Avoid: high-concentration alcohol toners, aggressive foaming cleansers, over-exfoliation, hot water cleansing.
Morning formula: cream cleanser → hyaluronic serum on damp skin → ceramide moisturizer → SPF.
Night formula: gentle cleanser → optional hydrating serum → rich barrier-repair cream; retinol only if tolerated, 2-3 nights weekly.

Combination Skin — Best Practices
Characteristics: mixed oil production across face; cheeks may be normal or dry while T-zone is oily.
Goals: balance T-zone without drying cheeks; flexible layering by zone when needed.
Recommended ingredients: niacinamide, hyaluronic acid, light BHA on T-zone only, gel-cream moisturizers.
Product textures: gentle foaming or gel cleanser, lightweight moisturizer, broad-spectrum sunscreen.
Zone approach: apply BHA or oil-control serum to T-zone; use richer moisturizer on dry cheeks if needed.
Morning formula: balanced cleanser → niacinamide serum → lightweight moisturizer → SPF.
Night formula: cleanser → treatment serum (BHA T-zone or hydrating serum cheeks) → moisturizer.

Sensitive Skin — Best Practices
Characteristics: reactive barrier, redness, stinging; may overlap with rosacea-prone or dry skin.
Goals: strengthen barrier, minimize irritants, introduce one new product at a time, patch test.
Recommended ingredients: ceramides, centella asiatica, allantoin, colloidal oatmeal, panthenol, azelaic acid (gentle option for redness and blemishes).
Product textures: fragrance-free gentle cleanser, mineral sunscreen (zinc oxide), simple short-ingredient moisturizers.
Avoid: essential oils, denatured alcohol, strong fragrances, high-strength acids without patch test, multiple new actives at once.
Morning formula: micellar or cream cleanser → soothing serum → barrier moisturizer → mineral SPF.
Night formula: gentle cleanser → optional azelaic acid or niacinamide → barrier cream.

Normal Skin — Best Practices
Characteristics: balanced, few major concerns; focus on prevention and maintenance.
Goals: daily SPF, antioxidant protection, gradual anti-aging prevention if desired.
Recommended ingredients: vitamin C (morning), niacinamide, hyaluronic acid, retinol (night, preventive), ceramides.
Morning formula: gentle cleanser → vitamin C serum → moisturizer → SPF 30+.
Night formula: cleanser → retinol or peptide serum (2-3 nights weekly to start) → moisturizer.

Universal Rules (All Skin Types)
- Cleanse twice daily maximum unless medically advised otherwise.
- Daily sunscreen is the most evidence-backed anti-aging and hyperpigmentation prevention step.
- Introduce one new active ingredient at a time; wait 2-4 weeks before adding another.
- Patch test behind ear or inner arm for 24-48 hours if sensitive or reactive.
- Dehydration can affect any skin type — hyaluronic acid plus moisturizer helps oily skin too.

When to Refer to a Dermatologist
Cystic acne, spreading rash, suspected infection, severe eczema or psoriasis flares, persistent irritation despite gentle routine, or any condition requiring prescription treatment.
