Glow Beauty Co. — Ingredient FAQ

Niacinamide
- Helps with oil control, redness, and the appearance of enlarged pores.
- Generally well tolerated by oily, combination, and acne-prone skin.
- Can be used morning and/or night.

Vitamin C (L-Ascorbic Acid)
- Antioxidant that supports brighter-looking skin and helps with uneven tone.
- Best used in the morning before sunscreen.
- Start with lower frequency if you have sensitive skin.

Can I use Niacinamide with Vitamin C?
- Yes, they can be used together in many routines.
- For sensitive skin: use Vitamin C in the morning and Niacinamide at night to reduce irritation risk.
- For tolerant skin: layer thinnest texture first; both can be used in the same routine.

Retinol
- Supports smoother-looking skin and helps reduce the appearance of fine lines over time.
- Introduce slowly: 2-3 nights per week, then increase as tolerated.
- Always use sunscreen during the day when using retinol at night.
- Avoid combining with strong acids on the same night when first starting.

Salicylic Acid (BHA)
- Oil-soluble exfoliant helpful for clogged pores and acne-prone skin.
- Suitable for many oily and combination skin types.
- May be too strong for very dry, compromised, or highly sensitive barriers.

Hyaluronic Acid
- Hydrating ingredient that attracts moisture to the skin.
- Works across skin types, including oily and dry skin.
- Apply to slightly damp skin, then seal with moisturizer.

Ceramides
- Support barrier repair and help reduce moisture loss.
- Excellent for dry, sensitive, and post-treatment skin.

Sunscreen
- Daily SPF is essential when using Vitamin C, retinol, or exfoliating acids.
- Mineral sunscreens (zinc oxide, titanium dioxide) are often preferred for sensitive skin.

When to See a Dermatologist
- Severe or cystic acne, infections, persistent rashes, swelling, or suspected allergic reactions.
- We provide general skincare guidance only, not medical diagnosis or treatment.
