Glow Beauty Co. — Ingredient Compatibility and Layering Guide

Purpose
Help the AI answer "can I use X with Y?" and build safe, effective routines. Based on common dermatology and cosmetic chemistry practice.

General Layering Rules
- Apply thinnest to thickest texture.
- Water-based before oil-based.
- Wait 30-60 seconds between layers if using low-pH actives (optional, helps sensitive users).
- Sunscreen is always the last morning step.
- When in doubt, separate irritating actives to different times of day.

Safe and Common Combinations
Niacinamide + Hyaluronic Acid: yes, same routine; hydrate and balance.
Niacinamide + Vitamin C: yes for most people; both morning or vitamin C AM / niacinamide PM for very sensitive skin.
Niacinamide + Retinol: yes; niacinamide may reduce irritation; can layer if tolerated or use niacinamide AM / retinol PM.
Hyaluronic Acid + Ceramide Moisturizer: yes; apply HA on damp skin, seal with ceramides.
Vitamin C + Sunscreen: yes; vitamin C boosts antioxidant defense, SPF is essential.
Azelaic Acid + Niacinamide: yes; good for acne, redness, and marks.
Peptides + Most Actives: generally compatible; peptides in moisturizer or serum.

Combinations Requiring Caution (Beginners)
Retinol + Salicylic Acid (BHA): avoid same night when starting; alternate nights or BHA AM / retinol PM only if experienced and tolerant.
Retinol + Glycolic/Lactic Acid (AHA): do not use same night initially; alternate evenings.
Retinol + Benzoyl Peroxide: can deactivate retinol and increase irritation — alternate nights or BP morning / retinol night with caution.
Vitamin C (L-Ascorbic Acid) + Direct Acids (AHA/BHA): may irritate sensitive skin if layered at same time; separate AM/PM.
Multiple Strong Actives Same Night: avoid — e.g., retinol + BHA + benzoyl peroxide together when beginning.

Often Used on Alternate Nights
Night A: retinol + moisturizer.
Night B: BHA or AHA + moisturizer.
Night C: recovery only — gentle cleanser + barrier moisturizer (recommended weekly rest).

Same Routine — Experienced Users Only
Retinol after BHA: only if skin is acclimated over months; not for beginners.
Niacinamide before or after vitamin C: order by texture; both stable in modern formulations.

Ingredients That Increase Sun Sensitivity
Retinol, AHAs, BHAs, benzoyl peroxide — require daily broad-spectrum SPF 30+.
Failure to use SPF causes worsening hyperpigmentation and aging.

pH Considerations (Simplified)
Low-pH vitamin C and AHAs: use before neutral moisturizers.
Niacinamide tolerates a wide pH range in contemporary formulas.
Cleanser always comes first.

Pregnancy and Nursing — General Avoid List (Confirm with Doctor)
Retinoids (retinol, adapalene, tretinoin OTC discussion — avoid unless physician says otherwise).
High-strength salicylic acid peels and large-area high % BHA.
Hydroquinone without medical supervision.

Patch Testing Protocol
Apply new product to inner arm or behind ear once daily for 3-5 days.
If no reaction, apply to small face area (jawline) for 3 days.
Then full face application.

Signs to Stop and Reset Routine
Persistent burning, swelling, hives, oozing, or worsening breakouts unlike normal mild purging.
Action: stop all actives, use gentle cleanser + barrier moisturizer + SPF for 5-7 days.

Purging vs Breakout (Retinol / BHA)
Purging: small bumps in usual acne areas, improves within 4-6 weeks.
Reaction: widespread itching, rash, or breakouts in new areas — discontinue and seek advice.

Recommended Starter Stacks by Goal
Acne prevention stack: cleanser + niacinamide (daily) + BHA (2-3 nights/week) + moisturizer + SPF.
Anti-aging stack: vitamin C (AM) + retinol (PM 2-3 nights/week) + moisturizer + SPF.
Dark spot stack: vitamin C (AM) + azelaic acid or niacinamide (PM) + SPF mandatory.
Dry barrier stack: hyaluronic acid + ceramide moisturizer AM/PM; add retinol only after barrier stable.

AI Response Guidelines
When customer asks compatibility: give clear yes/no with timing guidance (same routine vs alternate nights).
When uncertain about sensitivity: recommend separating actives AM/PM.
Never claim medical certainty — suggest dermatologist for prescription interactions or diagnosed conditions.
