Glow Beauty Co. — Routine Formulas by Skin Type and Concern

Purpose
Ready-to-use morning and night routine frameworks. Match catalog products to each step. Always personalize based on customer skin type, concern, and tolerance.

Standard Layering Order
1. Cleanser (water-based)
2. Toner or essence (optional)
3. Water-based serums (hyaluronic acid, niacinamide)
4. Treatment serums (vitamin C morning; BHA, retinol, or azelaic night)
5. Eye cream (optional)
6. Moisturizer
7. Sunscreen (morning only — last step)

Oily + Acne-Prone — Prevention Routine
Morning: gel cleanser → niacinamide serum → oil-free gel moisturizer → lightweight SPF 30+ (non-comedogenic).
Night: salicylic cleanser or gentle gel cleanser → BHA exfoliant serum (2-3 nights/week) OR niacinamide (other nights) → oil-free moisturizer.
Weekly add-on: clay mask on T-zone once; hydrocolloid patches on individual blemishes.

Dry + Sensitive — Barrier Repair Routine
Morning: cream or milk cleanser → hyaluronic serum on damp skin → ceramide moisturizer → mineral SPF.
Night: gentle cream cleanser → azelaic acid or niacinamide (start 2-3 nights/week) → rich ceramide cream.
Avoid: foaming sulfates, fragrance, same-night retinol and strong acids when beginning.

Combination — Balance Routine
Morning: gentle foaming cleanser → lightweight niacinamide serum → gel-cream moisturizer → SPF.
Night: cleanser → BHA on T-zone only (2-3 nights/week) → moisturizer (richer on cheeks if dry).
Optional: different moisturizers by zone.

Normal — Maintenance and Prevention
Morning: gentle cleanser → vitamin C serum → daily moisturizer → SPF 30+.
Night: cleanser → retinol 2-3 nights/week (other nights: hydrating serum) → moisturizer.

Aging Concerns (Fine Lines) — Evidence-Based Routine
Morning: gentle cleanser → vitamin C antioxidant serum → peptide or hydrating serum → moisturizer → SPF (mandatory with retinol use).
Night: cleanser → retinol serum or cream (start 2 nights/week) → ceramide moisturizer.
Eye area: peptide or low-strength retinol eye product if tolerated, applied before moisturizer.

Hyperpigmentation / Dark Spots Routine
Morning: cleanser → vitamin C serum → niacinamide (if separate product) → moisturizer → SPF 50 preferred.
Night: cleanser → azelaic acid OR alpha arbutin serum (alternate with retinol nights) → moisturizer.
Non-negotiable: daily sunscreen or spots darken faster.

Dehydrated Oily Skin Routine
Morning: gentle cleanser → hyaluronic acid on damp skin → lightweight gel moisturizer → SPF.
Night: cleanser → niacinamide → hyaluronic serum → gel moisturizer.
Key principle: dehydration worsens oiliness; do not skip moisturizer.

Sensitive / Redness-Prone Routine
Morning: fragrance-free gentle cleanser → centella or panthenol serum → barrier moisturizer → mineral sunscreen.
Night: gentle cleanser → azelaic acid 10% (often well tolerated) OR niacinamide → barrier cream.
Introduce one product every 2 weeks.

Teen Acne (Mild) — Simple Routine
Morning: gentle cleanser → oil-free moisturizer → SPF.
Night: cleanser → BHA treatment (2-3 nights/week) → moisturizer.
Avoid overloading with too many actives. Dermatologist for moderate-severe acne.

Minimal Routine (3 Steps — Beginner)
Morning: cleanser → moisturizer → sunscreen.
Night: cleanser → single treatment serum → moisturizer.
Best for: routine fatigue, sensitive skin reset, or new users. Add actives one at a time after 2-4 weeks.

Double Cleansing (When to Use)
Evening only, when wearing waterproof sunscreen, makeup, or heavy SPF.
Step 1: cleansing oil or balm (fragrance-free for sensitive).
Step 2: water-based gentle cleanser.
Not required for everyone — oily acne-prone may prefer single gentle cleanse if no heavy makeup.

How to Introduce Actives Safely
Week 1-2: cleanser, moisturizer, SPF only.
Week 3+: add niacinamide or hyaluronic acid daily.
Week 5+: add BHA or retinol 2 nights per week.
Week 7+: increase frequency if no irritation (redness, peeling, stinging).
If irritation occurs: pause actives 3-5 days, use barrier moisturizer only, then restart slower.

Product Recommendation Rules for AI
- Map each step to a real catalog product.
- Explain why each product fits the customer's stated skin type and concern.
- Offer a simpler 3-step version if customer seems overwhelmed.
- Always mention SPF for morning routines.
- Ask skin type if missing before finalizing moisturizer and treatment choices.
