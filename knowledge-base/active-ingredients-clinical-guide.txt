Glow Beauty Co. — Active Ingredients Clinical Guide

Purpose
Evidence-based reference for key skincare actives: what they do, typical concentrations, how often to use, and safety notes. Use to justify product recommendations.

Salicylic Acid (BHA)
Best for: acne, clogged pores, blackheads, oily skin, uneven texture.
Mechanism: oil-soluble beta hydroxy acid penetrates pores to dissolve sebum and dead cells.
Typical concentrations: 0.5-2% leave-on; 2% common in serums and spot treatments; cleansers rinse off (less intense).
Frequency: start 2-3 nights per week; increase to every other night if tolerated.
Pair with: niacinamide (different routines or times), lightweight moisturizer, SPF by day.
Avoid same night when starting: retinol, benzoyl peroxide, strong AHAs.
Caution: dry, cracked, or eczematous skin — may irritate; pregnancy high-dose body peels need physician guidance.

Benzoyl Peroxide
Best for: inflammatory acne, antibacterial action, body acne washes.
Typical concentrations: 2.5-5% (2.5% often as effective with less irritation).
Frequency: short-contact cleanser daily OR leave-on gel every other night when starting.
Caution: bleaches fabric; dryness and peeling common; do not combine with retinol on same night initially.
Patch test if sensitive.

Niacinamide (Vitamin B3)
Best for: oil control, redness, pore appearance, barrier support, mild hyperpigmentation, acne-prone skin.
Typical concentrations: 2-10% (5-10% common in serums).
Frequency: once or twice daily; very well tolerated across skin types.
Combines well with: vitamin C (most skin types), retinol (alternate or layered if tolerated), hyaluronic acid.
One of the safest versatile actives for combination and sensitive skin.

Vitamin C (L-Ascorbic Acid and Derivatives)
Best for: antioxidant protection, brightening, hyperpigmentation support, collagen synthesis support.
Typical concentrations: L-ascorbic acid 10-20% at pH below 3.5 for efficacy; derivatives (SAP, THD) gentler.
Frequency: morning before sunscreen for antioxidant synergy with UV protection.
Storage: airtight, opaque packaging; L-ascorbic acid oxidizes.
Caution: stinging on very sensitive skin — try derivatives or lower frequency.

Retinol (Vitamin A — OTC)
Best for: fine lines, texture, acne prevention, hyperpigmentation over time.
Typical concentrations: start 0.2-0.3%; intermediate 0.5%; advanced 1% if tolerated.
Frequency: 2 nights per week for 2 weeks, then every other night; always SPF by day.
Do not combine when starting: BHA, AHA, benzoyl peroxide on same night.
Side effects: peeling, dryness, purging first 4-6 weeks — normal if mild.
Pregnancy: avoid retinoids unless physician approves.

Azelaic Acid
Best for: acne, redness, rosacea-prone skin, post-acne marks, uneven tone.
Typical concentration: 10% OTC serum or cream.
Frequency: once or twice daily; among the gentler acne and redness actives.
Combines well with: niacinamide, hyaluronic acid, mineral SPF.
Good option when customer cannot tolerate retinol or BHA.

Glycolic Acid and Lactic Acid (AHA)
Glycolic: smaller molecule, stronger — oily, normal, texture concerns.
Lactic: gentler, hydrating — dry, sensitive, dullness.
Typical concentrations: 5-10% leave-on for beginners; cleansers/toners lower exposure.
Frequency: 2-3 nights per week; not same night as retinol when beginning.
Requires: strict SPF; increases sun sensitivity.

Hyaluronic Acid
Best for: hydration all skin types including oily and dehydrated skin.
Mechanism: humectant — draws water; must seal with moisturizer.
Application: on damp skin; layer moisturizer on top.
MW variants: multi-weight formulas hydrate different skin layers.

Ceramides, Cholesterol, Fatty Acids
Best for: barrier repair, dry skin, sensitive skin, post-active recovery.
Use: moisturizer step morning and night; essential after retinol or exfoliation nights.
Look for: ceramide NP, AP, EOP in ingredient lists.

Peptides
Best for: supportive anti-aging, firmness appearance, gentle alternative for retinol-sensitive users.
Use: serum or moisturizer; combine with SPF and antioxidants for prevention routine.

Tea Tree Oil
Best for: spot treatment on individual blemishes — not full-face undiluted.
Caution: irritant potential; patch test; dilute in carrier for sensitive skin.

Zinc Oxide and Titanium Dioxide (Mineral SPF)
Best for: sensitive, redness-prone, post-procedure skin; broad-spectrum UV protection.
Daily use: the single most evidence-backed step for aging and spot prevention.

Alpha Arbutin and Tranexamic Acid
Best for: hyperpigmentation, melasma support (tranexamic acid — ongoing research support).
Use with: vitamin C, niacinamide, SPF; expect 8-12 week timelines.

Centella Asiatica (Cica)
Best for: soothing, barrier support, post-irritation, sensitive skin.
Use: toner or serum in redness or post-active recovery routines.

Ingredient Selection by Concern (Quick Reference)
Acne prevention: salicylic acid, benzoyl peroxide, niacinamide, azelaic acid.
Aging: retinol, peptides, vitamin C, SPF.
Dark spots: vitamin C, azelaic acid, niacinamide, retinol, SPF.
Dryness: ceramides, hyaluronic acid, squalane, glycerin.
Redness: azelaic acid, niacinamide, centella, mineral SPF.
Oily shine: niacinamide, salicylic acid, lightweight gel moisturizer, clay mask weekly.

Medical Disclaimer
OTC skincare supports mild to moderate concerns. Prescription retinoids, antibiotics, hormonal therapy, and procedural treatments require a dermatologist.
